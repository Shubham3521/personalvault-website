01 — Overview

The short version

PersonalVault is a self-hosted photo and video backup device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to third parties. All data is stored exclusively on your own device in your own home.

When you use the PersonalVault app, your photos travel encrypted from your phone directly to the device you physically own. That device lives on your home network. We provide the software and, optionally, an encrypted relay for remote access — but we never see, read, or store your photos.

02 — App Permissions

What the app accesses and why

The PersonalVault mobile app requests the following permissions:

Permission Why we need it
🖼️Photo Library To back up your photos and videos to your personal device
📷Camera For QR code scanning during initial setup and login
📶Network access To connect to your self-hosted PersonalVault device
🔔Notifications To notify you of backup status and completion
⏱️Background refresh To run automatic backups in the background (iOS)
03 — Data Collection

We collect nothing

PersonalVault does not collect any personal data. Your photos, videos, login credentials, and server information are stored only on your phone and your own PersonalVault device. We have zero access to any of this data.

We do not log usage metrics, session data, or any information about what you store or how you use the app. There are no analytics SDKs, crash reporters, or advertising frameworks embedded in our software.

04 — Analytics & Tracking

Zero analytics, zero tracking

PersonalVault contains no analytics SDKs (no Firebase Analytics, no Amplitude, no Mixpanel), no advertising frameworks, and no crash reporting services that transmit data off-device.

We do not track your usage patterns, feature usage, navigation, or behavior in any way. Your activity within the app stays entirely on your device.

05 — Remote Access

How remote access works

When you access your photos away from home, the PersonalVault app uses an encrypted relay tunnel to route the connection between your phone and your device. This relay is solely a transport mechanism — we route the encrypted packets, but we never decrypt, inspect, or store them.

Think of it like a private postal service that delivers sealed envelopes. We deliver the envelope; we have no idea what's inside and no way to open it.

06 — Your Hardware

Your device, your control

PersonalVault connects only to the server URL you configure — the IP address of your physical device. We do not operate or control your device. It's your hardware, running in your home, under your control.

Even if PersonalVault as a company ceased to exist, your photos would remain safely on the hardware you own. The device runs open-source software and continues to work completely independently.

07 — Changes

Policy updates

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. When we do, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. For material changes, we'll send an email to registered users. Continued use of PersonalVault after a change constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
